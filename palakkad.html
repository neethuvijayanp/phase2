Palakkad

About District

Palakkad or the 'Gateway to Kerala' is truly a paradise for the nature lovers. Situated at the foothills of the mighty Western Ghats, this scenic land is jotted with lush and evergreen vegetation all throughout, and is adored as the 'Granary of Kerala'. Palakkad has a tropical wet and dry climate. Temperatures remain moderate throughout the year, with the exception being March and April, the hottest months. A very high amount of precipitation is received in Palakkad, mainly due to the South-West monsoon. The district is also the land of Palmyrahs and its economy is primarily agricultural. Palakkad is also known as the rice bowl of Kerala.

Must Visit Places

Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary

The wildlife sanctuary, which had an area of 285 square kilometres was established in 1973. It is in the Sungam range of hills between the Anaimalai Hills and Nelliampathy Hills.

Malampuzha Dam

Malampuzha Dam is the largest dam and reservoirs in Kerala. The dam is 355 feet high and crosses the Malampuzha River, a tributary of Bharathappuzha. Around the reservoir of the dam are gardens and amusement parks. Boating facilities are available on the lake.

Nelliyampathy

Nelliyampathy is a fascinating hill station at a height of 467 to 1,572 m above sea level. This hilly village is the delight of trekkers.

Rock Garden

This impressive collection of artwork is conceived by renowned sculptor Padmasree Neck Chand Saini. The artifacts displayed here are made of waste materials such as used plastic bottles, waste pieces of Tiles, Stones, Electricity Fuse Carriers, Power Insulators.

Silent Valley

The national park is one of the last undisturbed tracts of South Western Ghats mountain rain forests and tropical moist evergreen forest in India. It is located in the Nilgiri hills and has some rare species of flora and fauna.

Jain Temple

The Jain temple made of beautiful granite stones and featuring images of the Jain Tirthankaras and Yakshinis. It is one of the few temples in India where remains of Jain religion can still be found.

Meenvallam Waterfalls

Meenvallam waterfalls area is located in Olvakkode Forest Range and 34Km from Palakkad. This spot is located in Kalladikkode Mala. 5 step waterfalls is the main attraction of this area and also the area is surrounded with thick forest.
